excerpt: 'This paper studies adaptive peer-referral recruitment, where limited referral resources must be allocated across multiple rounds and current decisions affect both the number and covariates of future recruits. We propose Generative Frontier Planning, a model-based planner that learns covariate-dependent referral dynamics and uses a latent coverage value surrogate to make future-frontier planning tractable. By replacing per-step Monte Carlo sampling with deterministic backups and exploiting diminishing returns for greedy allocation, our method improves recruitment performance over random, reinforcement-learning, and population-level dynamic-programming baselines.'
